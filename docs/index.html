Screenplay Cards

A FREE Mac app for organizing your screenplay — beats, scenes, structure — using digital index cards.

One way to use it

The front of each card holds a single sentence: the beat. What happens in this scene, distilled to one line. You build out all your beats, drop in act header cards to mark your structure, then drag everything around until the story makes sense.

Once the order feels right, you flip each card and write the back — everything you’ll need when you actually sit down to write that scene: character details, dialogue fragments, the emotional arc, visual ideas, whatever helps.

When you’re done, hit outline. You get a complete step outline — every beat numbered and in order, with your notes beneath each one. Take that into your screenwriting software and write.

That’s one workflow. Others use the front for scene headings and the back for scene details, or just as a way to think through structure visually. Use it however it serves your process.

What it does

Two-sided cards

Beat or scene title on the front, notes on the back. Rich text formatting in notes. Flip individual cards or all at once.

Color coding

Accent color bars and fill colors per card, with a per-project legend. Spotlight any color to see where it falls across your outline.

Act headers & filtering

Name a card ACT ONE and the app detects it automatically. A filter strip lets you isolate any act. Collapse acts to declutter the board.

Limbo panel

A holding area for cards you’ve cut but aren’t ready to delete. Park them on the right side of the board and restore anytime.

Multi-select

Select any number of cards at once to batch delete or move them to Limbo. Cmd+A selects all visible cards.

Card linking

Connect cards bidirectionally to track relationships across your outline — parallel storylines, callbacks, cause and effect.

Zoom & views

Grid or list view. Drag the zoom slider from 40% to 150%. Toggle column or row flow. Notes preview on card fronts without flipping.

Exports

Step outline with notes and color legend, beat sheet, or scene summary by act. All exports open in Preview with the full macOS print dialog.

Native file saving

Projects save as .screenplaycards files. Works with iCloud, Dropbox, and Google Drive. Auto-saves while you work.

Getting started

1

Download the right version

M1, M2, M3, or M4 Mac — use Apple Silicon. Older Mac (pre-2021) — use Intel. Not sure? Apple menu → About This Mac and look for “Apple M” or “Intel”.

2

Install the app

Open the .dmg file and drag Screenplay Cards into your Applications folder.

3

Save your project

Use File → Save (Cmd+S) to save your project as a .screenplaycards file. Store it anywhere — iCloud, Dropbox, your Desktop.

About

Screenplay Cards was designed and built by Peter Carrillo, a screenwriter who should have been working on his screenplay’s structure but instead made an app no one asked for that helps screenwriters organize screenplay structure. The code was written with the assistance of AI tools. It is offered freely to any writer who finds it useful. It is open source — modify it, improve it, pass it on.
