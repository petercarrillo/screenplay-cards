A digital index card system for organizing your screenplay. Built for writers, by a writer.

What it is

Screenplay Cards is a single HTML file that runs entirely in your browser — no installation, no account, no internet required after download. It replicates the classic index card workflow screenwriters have used for decades, with a few things physical cards can never do.

▣

Two-sided cards

Scene title on the front, notes on the back. Flip individual cards or all at once.

■

Color coding

Multi-color accent bars and fill colors with a per-project legend. Spotlight any color to see where it falls in the script.

□

Act filtering

Name a card ACT ONE and exclude it from scene numbering — the app detects it as a structural divider and builds a filter strip automatically.

◇

Multiple projects

Keep different screenplays in separate project tabs, each with its own cards and color legend.

◆

Fit all view

Scale every card to fit the screen at once for a true bird's eye view of your whole structure.

●

Outline export

Generate a clean numbered scene outline — titles as headings, notes as body text — ready to print or save as PDF.

One way to use it

The front of each card holds a single sentence: the beat. What happens in this scene, distilled to one line. You build out all your beats, drop in act header cards to mark your structure, then drag everything around until the story makes sense.


Once the order feels right, you flip each card and write the back — everything you'll need when you actually sit down to write that scene: character details, dialogue fragments, the emotional arc, visual ideas, whatever helps.


When you're done, hit outline. You get a complete step outline — every beat numbered and in order, with your notes beneath each one. Take that into your screenwriting software and write.


That's one workflow. Others use the front for scene headings and the back for scene details, or just as a way to think through structure visually. Use it however it serves your process.

Getting started

1

Download the app

Click the download button above. You get a single file: screenplay-cards.html

2

Put it somewhere that syncs

Move it into your iCloud Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive folder so it's always backed up. Always open it from the same location.

3

Open it in your browser

Double-click the file. It opens in Safari, Chrome, Firefox, or Edge. No installation needed.

4

Back up regularly

Hit export in the toolbar every so often. It saves a .json file with all your cards that you can restore at any time with import.

About

Screenplay Cards was built in April 2026 through a collaborative conversation between a writer and an AI — Peter Carrillo and Claude (Anthropic). It started as a personal tool to assist in the writing of screenplays, and is offered freely to any writer who finds it useful. It is open source — modify it, improve it, pass it on.


Built with HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. No frameworks. No server. No internet required. 819 lines of code in a single file.
