Screenplay Cards

A Mac app for organizing your screenplay — beats, scenes, structure — using digital index cards.

Download for Mac Apple Silicon — M1, M2, M3, M4 Download for Mac Intel — pre-2021 Macs

Actively developed — the app checks for updates automatically each time it launches.

Windows version coming soon.

One way to use it

The front of each card holds a single sentence: the beat. What happens in this scene, distilled to one line. You build out all your beats, drop in act header cards to mark your structure, then drag everything around until the story makes sense.

Once the order feels right, you flip each card and write the back — everything you’ll need when you actually sit down to write that scene: character details, dialogue fragments, the emotional arc, visual ideas, whatever helps.

When you’re done, hit outline. You get a complete step outline — every beat numbered and in order, with your notes beneath each one. Take that into your screenwriting software and write.

That’s one workflow. Others use the front for scene headings and the back for scene details, or just as a way to think through structure visually. Use it however it serves your process.

What it does

Two-sided cards

Beat or scene title on the front, notes on the back. Rich text formatting in notes. Flip individual cards or all at once.

Color coding

Multi-color accent bars and fill colors with a per-project legend. Spotlight any color to see where it falls across your script.

Act filtering

Name a card ACT ONE and the app detects it as a structural divider and builds a filter strip automatically.

Native file saving

Each project saves as a .screenplaycards file anywhere on your Mac. Open, close, and manage projects like any other document.

Fit all view

Scale every card to fit the screen at once — a true bird’s eye view of your whole structure.

Outline & beat sheet

Generate a numbered step outline with notes, or a clean beat sheet with titles only. Both respect active act filters.

Getting started

1

Download the right version

M1, M2, M3, or M4 Mac — use Apple Silicon. Older Mac (pre-2021) — use Intel. Not sure? Apple menu → About This Mac and look for “Apple M” or “Intel”.

2

Install the app

Open the .dmg file and drag Screenplay Cards into your Applications folder.

3

First launch

Because the app isn’t signed with an Apple certificate, Mac will warn you the first time. Right-click the app → Open to bypass it. You only need to do this once.

4

Save your project

Use File → Save (Cmd+S) to save your project as a .screenplaycards file. Store it anywhere — iCloud, Dropbox, your Desktop.

The app checks for updates automatically on each launch. When a new version is available you’ll get a prompt to download and install it — no need to come back here.
